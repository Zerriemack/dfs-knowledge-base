# Analyzing an Organizing System: Pro Football Reference

## Overview

Pro Football Reference is a public football statistics knowledge base that organizes NFL history into connected pages and searchable tables. It supports factual lookups and detailed research into players, teams, games, and records across many seasons.

## Primary Resources

The primary resources are player pages, team pages, season pages, game logs, box scores, draft pages, award records, leaderboards, and coaching and franchise records. A quarterback's player page includes career passing statistics, seasonal totals, and links to individual game logs. A team season page combines its roster, schedule, results, standings, and statistical leaders. A box score page documents one game through scoring summaries, team totals, and individual performances. Leaderboard pages organize categories such as passing yards, passing touchdowns, rushing yards, and receiving yards. Together, these resources create a detailed record of professional football history.

## Primary Interactions

The system supports research, browsing, comparison, fact-checking, historical lookup, and statistical analysis. Users can check a player's career totals, compare two seasons, look up a team's schedule, find a playoff box score, or verify a player's draft position or award history. Sortable tables make it possible to compare players by a selected category. Links also support exploratory movement from one related page to another, which is useful when a lookup develops into a broader research question.

## Classification System

Pro Football Reference uses hierarchical, faceted, and database-driven classification. Its hierarchy lets users move from league history to a season, then to a team, player, or game. Its faceted structure organizes information by year, team, position, statistic, leader category, split, and game log. Many database-generated tables can also be sorted by statistical columns.

This approach is effective for football fans, researchers, analysts, DFS players, journalists, and others who need accurate historical data. The pages can feel dense to a casual user because the tables present many categories at once. For users who know what they want to find, however, the structure makes detailed research fast and consistent.

## Relationships Between Resources

Relationships are represented through hyperlinks, tables, and page context. Player names, teams, seasons, games, awards, and drafts link to related records. Tables connect players to teams and seasons, while game logs connect individual performances to specific games. Box scores bring together teams, players, coaches, scores, and statistics. Leaderboards connect players across different eras.

For example, a user can start on a team's 2023 season page, click a player, open that player's game log, and then move to a box score from one of those games. This path shows how the system connects resources across several levels while keeping each page focused.

## Conclusion

Pro Football Reference is an effective organizing system because it supports both direct lookup and exploratory research. It organizes football history through linked pages, sortable tables, and category-based navigation, allowing users to move from broad historical records to specific performances without losing context.
