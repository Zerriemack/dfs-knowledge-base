# Late Swap Process

![[late-swap-process.png]]

Late swap is a structured reassessment of unlocked roster spots after a slate begins. It is not an automatic reason to change players. The decision should combine new information with the lineup's current position, remaining salary, observed ownership, and the range of outcomes still available.

## Reassess the Lineup

Start with injury and availability news. Confirm active status, expected limitations, starting lineups, and role changes before comparing alternatives. Then evaluate how early players performed. A lineup that is well ahead of expectations may benefit from retaining stronger projected and more popular options. A lineup far behind often needs lower-owned ceiling outcomes, provided those alternatives remain viable plays.

Ownership context matters at the lineup level. If an early popular player failed and the roster faded him, preserving a solid late construction can protect that advantage. If an early stack failed, swapping to a duplicated late combination rarely repairs the lineup's path to the top.

### A Repeatable Checklist

Before saving a swap, ask:

- Does new injury news change minutes, touches, targets, or starting status?
- How much salary remains, and can eligible players be moved between flexible roster spots?
- Which game scripts support the available combinations?
- Does the swap improve uniqueness or create unnecessary projection loss?
- Is the original build still aligned with the contest's payout structure?

Keep later-starting players in flexible slots whenever possible. This preserves options across positions and makes [[salary-allocation]] easier when news arrives. Review [[ownership-and-leverage]] before moving away from a popular play solely because it is popular.

Staying with the original lineup is correct when no material news has changed, the early results fit the planned game scripts, and the alternatives sacrifice meaningful projection for weak leverage. Every swap should improve the lineup's conditional path from its current state, not express discomfort with results already recorded.
