# Stacking and Correlation

![[assets/stacking-and-correlation.png]]

Correlation describes how one player's fantasy result changes the probability of another player's result. Tournament lineups benefit when several roster spots can reach their ceilings through the same game events. Instead of requiring unrelated players to post separate outlier scores, a correlated lineup can gain multiple scoring events from one productive environment.

## Building Around Passing Production

A quarterback paired with one or two pass catchers is the standard NFL stack. If the quarterback throws for four touchdowns, his receivers or tight end must account for much of that production. The stack connects their ceiling outcomes. Player roles still matter: a deep receiver offers a different path than a short-area target, while a rushing quarterback may need fewer teammates because some touchdowns come on the ground.

A bring-back adds an opponent from the same game. It is most useful when that player's production can force continued aggression from the quarterback's team. Bring-backs are not mandatory. A favored team with a high total may score efficiently without a competitive fourth quarter, and an opponent with concentrated volume is more appealing than one with uncertain usage.

### Evaluating a Game Environment

Review these factors before committing roster spots:

- team totals and the spread
- pace, play volume, and pass rate
- red-zone roles and target concentration
- injury-driven changes in opportunity
- weather or matchup effects on likely game scripts

> Correlation improves a lineup when the linked players also have sound roles, prices, and ceilings.

A popular game can still be the correct foundation, but [[ownership-and-leverage]] should guide the secondary pieces or construction. Salary also limits which combinations fit, so compare the stack against the alternatives described in [[salary-allocation]]. The goal is a lineup with a coherent path to a ceiling score, not the highest possible number of players from one game.
